NEET 2026 Re-Exam Answer Key Released by NTA
— Official Link, Download Steps & How to Challenge

📅 Published June 26, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 📋 Re-NEET held June 21, 2026
⚠️ Challenge Deadline: June 28, 2026 (11:50 PM IST)

If you want to dispute any answer, you have until June 28. Don't miss this window — ₹200 per question challenged, refunded if upheld.

The National Testing Agency (NTA) released the provisional answer key for the Re-NEET UG 2026 examination on June 25, 2026. The Re-NEET was conducted on June 21, 2026 across centres nationwide, and the answer key is available for all four paper codes: 50, 60, 70, and 80.

If you appeared for the Re-NEET, this is the most important thing you need to do right now — download your OMR sheet, cross-check it with the answer key, and if you spot any error, raise a challenge before June 28.

How to Download Your Answer Key + OMR Sheet

1

Go to neet.nta.nic.in — the official NTA NEET portal.

2

Click on "Display OMR Answer Sheet / Challenge Recorded Response and Answer Key".

3

Log in with your NEET 2026 Application Number and Password/Date of Birth.

4

Download your OMR sheet and cross-check every answer with the provisional answer key for your code.

5

If you find a discrepancy, use the challenge window on the same portal to raise objections.

How to Challenge the Answer Key

NTA allows candidates to challenge the provisional answer key if they believe a question has been marked incorrectly. Here's what you need to know:

  • Challenge fee: ₹200 per question challenged
  • Deadline: June 28, 2026, 11:50 PM IST
  • Payment mode: Online (credit/debit card, UPI, net banking)
  • Refund: Fee is refunded if the challenge is upheld by NTA's expert committee
  • How to challenge: Log in at neet.nta.nic.in → Select the question → Upload supporting evidence (NCERT page screenshot, reference text) → Pay and submit
Pro tip:

When raising a challenge, always attach a scanned page from your NCERT textbook or a credible reference. Challenges backed by evidence from standard textbooks (especially NCERT) have a much higher chance of being upheld.

What Happens After the Challenge Window Closes?

Once the challenge window closes on June 28, NTA will review all objections with a panel of subject experts. The final answer key will be released after this review. NEET 2026 results will then be declared based on the final answer key — likely within 2–3 weeks of the challenge window closing.

Marking Scheme Reminder

  • ✅ Correct answer: +4 marks
  • ❌ Wrong answer: −1 mark
  • ⬜ Unattempted: 0 marks

Use the answer key to calculate your expected score before the official result is declared. This helps you plan your next steps — whether that's preparing for counselling, starting dropper prep, or something else entirely.

What If Your Score Is Lower Than Expected?

Don't spiral. Your provisional score is just a number right now, and counselling is still weeks away. If things look difficult, read our guide on what colleges you can get with 300–400 marks, or understand what your next steps should be depending on where your score lands.

Every year, thousands of students who thought they'd scored poorly end up with better results than expected after the final key is released — especially in Biology, where diagram-based questions often have multiple defensible answers.
