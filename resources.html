Resources

Free NEET Resources
That Actually Matter

📅 Updated June 2025·⏱ 5 min read

The internet is full of NEET resource lists. Most of them recommend everything. We recommend only what top scorers consistently mention — and we cut everything that's redundant or distracting.

Books (Non-Negotiable)

  • Biology: NCERT Class 11 & 12 (read every line, every diagram). No substitute exists.
  • Biology (Advanced): Trueman's Objective Biology — for MCQ practice after NCERT mastery
  • Physics: DC Pandey (Objective) — best for NEET-style questions
  • Chemistry (Physical): NCERT + N Avasthi for numericals
  • Chemistry (Organic): MS Chauhan — the gold standard for mechanism-based questions
  • Chemistry (Inorganic): NCERT only. Strictly NCERT.

Rule of thumb: Finish one book completely before buying another. Most students have 5 half-read books and wonder why their score doesn't improve.

Free YouTube Channels Worth Your Time

  • Physics Wallah: Biology and Chemistry fundamentals — Alakh Pandey's explanations are genuinely excellent
  • Vedantu NEET Made Ejee: Good for Biology doubt-solving
  • Unacademy NEET (free content): Useful for specific chapter explanations

Free Mock Tests

  • NTA's official practice platform (nta.ac.in) — closest to the actual exam interface
  • Embibe (free tier) — good analytics on wrong answers
  • Allen's free test series (registration required)

Tools for Studying Smarter

  • Anki (free) — spaced repetition flashcards. Especially powerful for Biology terminology
  • Forest app — focus timer with gamification. Prevents phone distraction during study blocks
  • Google Sheets — build your own chapter tracker: topic, completion %, revision count, weak areas

If You're Considering Structured Coaching

Free resources can take you far — but they have limits. The limit isn't content; it's accountability and direction. If you find yourself drifting between resources without clear progress, or struggling to stay consistent, structured coaching with personal mentorship may close the gap faster than any new YouTube channel or book.
