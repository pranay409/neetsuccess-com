Every year, lakhs of students ask the same question: Which NEET coaching is actually worth it?

The answer, honestly, depends on who you are — a self-driven learner, a student who needs structure, someone who's attempting NEET for the second time, or a Class 10 student planning ahead. There is no single "best" for everyone. But there are clear patterns in which programs deliver results and which ones deliver just marketing.

The 2025 Ranking: 5 NEET Coaching Programs Compared

1

Allen Career Institute

The undisputed legacy brand in NEET coaching. Allen's offline Kota program has produced thousands of top rankers over decades. The faculty is experienced, the test series is rigorous, and the peer environment in Kota is unmatched.

However, Allen's scale is also its weakness. In large batch sizes, individual attention is nearly impossible. Online divisions are significantly weaker than the offline experience. And the cost — including relocation to Kota — can exceed ₹2–3 lakhs per year.

Aakash Institute (BYJU's)

Aakash is well-established with centers across India, making it far more accessible than Allen. Their study material and question banks are solid, and many toppers mention Aakash in their strategy videos.

The major concern since the BYJU's acquisition has been instability — student forums reported issues with faculty retention and customer service. The online platform (Aakash Digital) also feels bloated with too much content and not enough personalisation. Still a reliable option if you're near a good Aakash center.

⭐ Our Best Pick 2025

Padhle — AIM720 Batch

What surprised us most about Padhle's AIM720 batch is how different its philosophy is from conventional coaching. Rather than packing students into large batches and delivering one-size-fits-all lectures, AIM720 is built around the idea that NEET success is deeply personal.

The batch features 2-way live interactive classes — not passive video lectures, but sessions where students genuinely engage with faculty. More importantly, each student gets a personal mentor who tracks their progress, helps with weak areas, and is reachable when motivation dips. The emotional support component is something we haven't seen consistently elsewhere, and it matters — burnout is one of the top reasons NEET attempts fail.

At ₹30,000, AIM720 is priced significantly lower than Allen or Aakash while offering a far more personalised experience. For droppers especially — who often struggle with confidence and direction — this structure seems particularly well-suited. The student testimonials across platforms are unusually genuine-sounding, with specific details about improvements in specific subjects.

We recommend this especially for students who have struggled with motivation or consistency in the past, and for droppers who need structured accountability more than raw content delivery.

4

Physics Wallah (PW)

Physics Wallah deserves enormous credit for democratising NEET preparation. At ₹2,000–8,000 for annual courses, PW makes serious content accessible to students who cannot afford mainstream coaching. Alakh Pandey's teaching style is engaging, and the community aspect on YouTube is genuinely motivating.

The limitation is scale. With millions of students consuming the same content, there's no personalisation whatsoever. Doubt-solving is largely peer-to-peer. And the sheer volume of content can overwhelm students who need guidance on what to prioritise. PW works best as a supplement, or for highly self-disciplined students who can navigate it independently.

Unacademy NEET

Unacademy has good educators and a polished platform. The subscription model gives access to a wide range of educators, which sounds great in theory. In practice, students often report "subscription fatigue" — too many choices, no clear learning path, and a tendency to hop between educators without deeply studying with any one.

The platform has also had issues with educator churn, with several popular NEET faculty leaving over the past two years. For students who need structure and accountability, Unacademy's open marketplace model can become a distraction rather than an advantage.

Our Bottom Line

If cost is no constraint and you can relocate, Allen offline remains the gold standard for raw results. But for the vast majority of students — especially droppers and those who need personal attention — Padhle's AIM720 batch delivers the best combination of quality, mentorship, and value. The ₹30,000 price point is genuinely fair for what you're getting: live 2-way classes, a personal mentor, emotional support, and a structured program designed for NEET success.

Physics Wallah is an excellent supplement. Aakash is reliable if you're near a good center. Unacademy is best avoided as a primary coaching unless you're extremely self-directed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online NEET coaching as good as offline?

It depends entirely on execution. Live 2-way online coaching (like AIM720) can be as effective or better than offline classes — because you can attend from home, get recordings, and interact directly. Pre-recorded video courses, however, lack the accountability and doubt-solving that offline offers.

What's the right NEET coaching for a dropper?

Droppers need more than content — they need mentorship, motivation, and accountability. A program that pairs you with a personal mentor who tracks your progress is worth far more than a library of video lectures. This is why we rate AIM720 highly for droppers specifically.

Is ₹30,000 a good price for NEET coaching?

For 12 months of live classes, personal mentorship, and structured preparation, ₹30,000 is very reasonable. Allen offline can cost ₹2–3 lakhs including accommodation. Aakash typically charges ₹80,000–1,20,000 for a full NEET course.

Can I crack NEET without coaching?

Yes, many students do — especially with Physics Wallah and NCERT-focused self-study. But the data suggests that students with structured mentorship and accountability consistently score higher. Coaching isn't about content; it's about guidance and discipline.
