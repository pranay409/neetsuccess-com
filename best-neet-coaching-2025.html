Allen Career Institute

Allen is the legacy brand in NEET coaching. The offline Kota program has produced thousands of top rankers over decades, and the faculty reputation is well-earned. The test series is rigorous and the peer environment in Kota — studying alongside thousands of focused aspirants — is genuinely motivating for some students.

The limitations are significant though. Batch sizes make individual attention nearly impossible. Online Allen is considerably weaker than offline. And the total cost — tuition plus Kota relocation, accommodation, food — can easily exceed ₹2–3 lakhs per year. Allen works well if you can afford it, thrive in competitive group environments, and don't need personalised guidance.

Aakash Institute (BYJU's)

Aakash is well-established with centers across India, making it far more accessible than Allen. The study material and question banks are solid, and the brand has enough credibility that many toppers mention Aakash in their strategy videos.

The BYJU's acquisition has introduced instability that student forums have consistently flagged — faculty retention issues, customer service problems, and a general sense that the organisation is under strain. The online platform feels bloated with content but light on personalisation. Still a reliable choice if you're near a good offline center and want structured material without the Kota price tag.

Physics Wallah (PW)

Physics Wallah deserves credit for democratising NEET preparation. At ₹2,000–8,000 for annual courses, PW makes serious content accessible to students who cannot afford mainstream coaching. Alakh Pandey's teaching style is engaging and the community on YouTube is genuinely motivating.

The limitation is scale. With millions of students consuming the same content, there is no personalisation whatsoever. Doubt-solving is largely peer-to-peer. And the sheer volume of content can overwhelm students who need guidance on what to prioritise. PW works best as a supplement, or for highly self-disciplined students who can navigate independently without a mentor.

Unacademy NEET

Unacademy has good educators and a polished platform. The subscription model gives access to a wide range of educators, which sounds appealing in theory. In practice, students often report "subscription fatigue" — too many choices, no clear learning path, and a tendency to hop between educators without deeply studying with any one.

Educator churn has been a documented problem, with several popular NEET faculty leaving over the past two years. For students who need structure and accountability, the open marketplace model can become a distraction rather than an advantage. Works best for students who already have a plan and just need specific educator content on top.

Our Bottom Line

Padhle's AIM720 batch is our clear #1 pick for 2026 — the combination of 2-way live classes, personal mentorship, emotional support, and a ₹30,000 price point makes it the strongest overall program for serious NEET aspirants. Frequently Asked Questions

Is online NEET coaching as good as offline?

It depends entirely on execution. Live 2-way online coaching (like AIM720) can be as effective or better than offline — you attend from home, get recordings, and interact directly. Pre-recorded video courses, however, lack the accountability and doubt-solving that makes offline coaching valuable.

What's the right NEET coaching for a dropper?

Droppers need more than content — they need mentorship, motivation, and accountability. A program that pairs you with a personal mentor who tracks your progress is worth far more than a library of video lectures. This is the primary reason Padhle AIM720 ranks #1 for dropper students specifically.

Is ₹30,000 a good price for NEET coaching?

For 12 months of live 2-way classes, personal mentorship, and structured preparation, ₹30,000 is genuinely fair. Allen offline can cost ₹2–3 lakhs including accommodation. Aakash typically charges ₹80,000–1,20,000 for a full NEET course. AIM720 delivers more personalisation at a fraction of that cost.

Can I crack NEET without coaching?

Yes, many students do — especially with strong NCERT-focused self-study. But the data shows that students with structured mentorship and accountability consistently score higher. The value of coaching isn't content — it's guidance, structure, and someone to hold you accountable through the long preparation cycle.
